Generic Model Distillation - Statistical Stability in Bayesian and Tree Model
Model distillation has been a popular method for producing interpretable machine learning, where an interpretable student model is produced to mimic the predictions made by the black box teacher model. However, the interpretation of a student model is sensitive to the datasets, and existing strategy for statistical stability is focus only on specific models. Therefore, we developed a generic approach for stable model distillation - we develop statistical tests to ensure enough samples are generated to distinguish different students and consider decision tree, falling rule list and symbolic regression for demonstrations. We also provide theoretical analysis by treating it as a Markov process.

Automaton Distillation for Non-Markovian Knowledge Transfer in Reinforcement Learning
Existing methods in knowledge transfer as policy distillation suffer from two weaknesses. First, the teacher and student must share the same state space; second, they rely on the Markovian assumptions. We proposed automaton distillation mitigate both issues. We introduce a new and effective method of non-Markovian knowledge transfer that distills knowledge from teacher automaton to student DQN, and experimented on a blind-craftsman environment to display high efficiency and better performance of our proposed automation distillation method.
